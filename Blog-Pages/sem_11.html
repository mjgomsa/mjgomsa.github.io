Walkshop #11: Walking Artist
Recreation

April 6, 2022

I had a hard time picking a single artist to do this Walkshop on. Initially, I thought I wanted to do an homage to Kubra Khademi's performance to highlight sexual harassment—but I genuinely had no idea how I would be able to make an armor to walk around Chapel Hill in—although I still want to do this in the future, I opted to recreate another artist's work.


I decided to pay homage to Janet Cardiff and George Bures Miller's “After Bahuhof”. The piece plays a lot with composition of videos, integrating the past into the present—a theme that I have explored a lot in my last few walk analyses.




In order to carry out this piece I first researched videos online posted by other users of “walking tours around chapel hill” and such; and my initial idea was to just carry out those same tours but on my own now. Yet, I decided to make this project a little more personal.


I went onto my camera roll and looked through my videos of college, not really knowing what I would find. I was pleasantly surprised to see so many videos of my friends doing silly things in different locations on campus, and decided to recreate Cardiff and Miller's work with those. With my upcoming graduation, I've started thinking a lot of my friends and the people who have made Chapel Hill feel like a home—and so I wanted my project recreation to focus on the people rather than the place.


Shooting these new videos, while playing the videos of my friends from a while back made my heart ache a little. There was an aspect of loneliness and nostalgia in the creation of this project that I didn't anticipate. Revisiting places and memories I have with the people I love the most, made me feel a little melancholic yet this project just really opened my heart to the integration of the past and the present.
