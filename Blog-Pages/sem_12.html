Walkshop #12: Counter-Tourism

April 13, 2022


I got admitted to UNC in 2018 yet I didn’t really tour the campus until my first week of classes. That first tour was something I will never forget—while I see many of the same landmarks every day, they cannot compare to the first time I saw them. Now as I senior I want to reflect on what it was like to see UNC through the eyes of a freshman.


The Walk/Tour:


I started my walk at the corner of the Inn, given that it is the first sign of University of North Carolina that I see, and tried to forget my way around the campus. I had never noticed that at that corner there is a map to the University and so I studied the map and tried to make my way to the Old Well, trying to forget my past knowledge.


On my way to the old well I came across the zig-zags that hold bikes—maybe it is because I’ve never ridden a bike on campus, but I had never noticed them before. I snapped a picture and saw a student place his bike on the zig-zag and lock it. I kept walking and decided to inspect the light poles closely—I had never seen the flower engravings on the middle of them and though that was cool.




My first time touring the campus I remember grabbing a copy of the Daily Tarheel so I decided it would be very fitting to grab one now and so I did. I kept walking until I reached the meantime coffeeshop area and noticed something new—a new sculpture. I grabbed a seat on one of the benches and decided to look at the turtle for a little bit before continuing with my tour.




I decided to head to the main quad—and wow. I’m not sure if it is because it’s spring or just that I’m looking at it with a new, nostalgic set of eyes, but Wilson library looked especially nice in the background. I walked towards it and decided to sit for a while on the quad…until I had a horrible sneezing attack due to my allergies so decided to head to the student stores.



I walked into the student store and headed to the pharmacy upstairs where I picked up some Benadryl for my allergies (a souvenir!) and then asked myself—“if this were my first time visiting campus, what would I get?”. I still don’t own any Carolina gear, so decided to treat myself to a sweatshirt. I headed outside and sat on the pit for a little while longer… taking it all in.
