Walkshop #7: Notes to the Novice Pedestrian

March 2, 2022


This walk shop instructs us to walk around a busy city or town-center. Involving the long talks we've had about flanneur and the response I made on Walk the Line, I decided to give this flanneuring a go. To be a flanneur means to be an observer— being invisible while being visible— a wallflower.

  • A lot of people walking to and from class (wearing backpacks)
  • People holding umbrellas in case of that 20% rain
  • A lot of people wearing sunglasses
  • A guy, probably in his twenties jogging while listening to music. He speeds up as he passes past me (the song he was listening to probably had a beat drop)
  • I walk past Epilogue, and decide to stop in for a little bit. I record the sounds of the coffeeshop—people talking, the barista banging mugs and the espresso machine. To the corner, I see a guy working on a paper on his laptop— midterms are coming up and he's probably getting ahead on work
  • A couple walks in front of me, holding hands— they seem very much in love. Probably the first few weeks of their relationship. Or maybe just still very much in love.
  • A mom and two little kids, getting a couple of sodas from Suttons. I smile at them and they smile back. I stick my tongue out to him in a jokingly manner and he copies and giggles shyly.
  • I keep walking and realize most people are walking closer to the display windows than to the road— why is that? Maybe to watch out for reckless drivers I think.

I decided to stop at Linda's and get a late-afternoon coffee while sitting outside. An old man, sitting across from me smiles and asks me how's my day going— “ very well, you?” I reply. “very well” he says politely. I decide to get out my sketchbook and discretely begin sketching out the man, yet not even 3 minutes later he gets up and leaves. I'd like to think he had to get home in time for dinner to his wife, and that his cute little grandkids were going to visit.

I sipped my coffee, listened to the audio and kept hanging at Linda's for a bit. I ordered my check and decided to head home. Very successful day as a flanneur overall
