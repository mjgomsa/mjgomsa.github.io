Response #2: The Modern Flâneur

February 6, 2022


Considering it's different definitions, what purpose could/does the flâneur serve today? Come up with an example of today's “flaneur”. Who/ where are they? What does flaneuring signify in contemporary society?

Times are most definitely different—especially depending of where in the world you are. Flâneur by definition is a French noun referring to a person, literally meaning "stroller", "lounger", "saunterer", or "loafer”. The United States is generally more fast paced than countries in Europe for instance. Take New York for instance, you will never see a New Yorker simply strolling the streets and observing—becoming one with the city; New Yorkers always have some place to be and something to do, and the act of just being does not resonate with them. The act of being a flâneur is entirely European and hence way more common to see them in Europe. I had the privilege to intern in Paris this past summer, and during my time there I encountered many flaneurs—people just going on walks, admiring their city, their culture and sitting down at coffeeshops.

Parisian flaneurs, in my opinion, have become café regulars since then. Coffee culture is at the essence of Paris. Unlike Starbucks in the US most just get a coffee to go, a Parisian café is designed to embrace culture and has been the home of artists, writers, intellectuals such as Hemmingway, Picasso, and Manet. This was a long time ago, but frequenting these coffeeshops over the summer and thinking back on this now, the flâneur culture is still very much alive: people sketching, observing, writing and just flanneur-ing.
